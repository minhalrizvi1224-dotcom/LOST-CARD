The Theory

The Bet of Belief

Every word you speak in a relationship is a card you play. You play without knowing you are in a game — until the cards are gone.

💜
Devotion
Your emotional investment. How much of yourself you have placed into this relationship.
Lost through habitual aggression in calm states, or investing before trust can hold the weight.
💙
Excitement
Your relational energy. The life inside the connection — curiosity, anticipation, aliveness.
Lost when unresolved conflicts stack faster than they are cleared, especially two aggressive moves in sequence.
💚
Presence
Your psychological availability. Whether the other person can count on you to actually show up.
Lost through repeated withdrawal — three silences, or staying in high-stress states too long.

The NLI tracks your neurological load in real time. Every move shifts your PFC, cortisol, and dopamine — and the simulation reads all three.

What's Inside

Built for precision.

Every feature maps to a real neurological or relational mechanism — not metaphor, math.

🧠

Neurological Load Index

NLI = (PFC × 0.4) + (Cortisol × 0.4) + (1 − Dopamine) × 0.2. Computed live from your choices. The single most important number in the simulation.

🃏

Three-Card System

Devotion, Excitement, and Presence track three independent dimensions of relational health. Each drops under specific, computable conditions.

♟️

Chess Engine

A minimax algorithm replays The Immortal Game (Anderssen, 1851) alongside your conversation. Your moves shift the position. Eval ≤ −7.5 is CHECKMATE.

📊

Session Report

Five-part analysis after every session: DSA data, psychological profile, mistakes report, chess report, and move-by-move replay. No session goes unread.

🎭

Relational Archetype

After every session, the system assigns your archetype — Calm Aggressor, Silent Accumulator, Flooded Fixer, Over-Investor, and more. Based on your actual move data.

💯

Health Score

A 0–100 composite score from card retention, trust, NLI, and harmony ratio. Graded A+ to F. Includes a one-line verdict on the session.

🔧

Pattern Interrupt

A once-per-session move that simulates genuine honesty. At trust ≥ 0.60 it resets the conflict stack. Below that threshold, it backfires — vulnerability at low trust reads as desperation.

💌

The Final Letter

When a session ends in collapse, the AI character writes a closing letter — from their perspective, about what they experienced in the conversation. Not analysis. A letter.

👁️

Repair Window Indicator

A live badge showing whether your cortisol stack can be cleared right now. OPEN means a SOFT move will land. CLOSED means you are too stressed to receive repair — cool down first.

🔬

Gottman Tone Vectors

Each message is classified beyond SOFT/AGGRESSIVE/SILENT into specific Gottman subtypes — Contempt, Vulnerability, Repair Attempt, Stonewalling, and more.

👻

Ghost Session

In Ex chat, the AI may show a typing indicator and go silent. Models the psychological experience of intermittent non-response — anticipation without resolution.

Architecture

7 data structures. 1 relationship.

All seven run simultaneously. Every move updates all of them at once.

01

Weighted DAG + Dijkstra

Hippocampal memory network. 22 nodes. Aggression degrades edges. Soft moves repair them. Exit path length = distance to decoupling.

02

LIFO Stack

Cortisol accumulation. Max depth 7. NLI-gated pop — repair only works when you are calm enough to receive it. Overflow loses Excitement.

03

Min-Heap Priority Queue

PFC corruption. At NLI ≥ 0.85, the amygdala overrides — AGGRESSIVE is ranked first by the stress-modified comparator, not by choice.

04

Singly Linked List

Default Mode Network — longing. Lost relational memories are orphaned pointers. Not freed. That is intentional. That is the architecture of longing.

05

Hash Map

Sovereign Key — identity and protected memory. Some truths in a relationship are only accessible through specific keys. O(1) lookup.

06

Finite State Machine

Phase transitions — HARMONY → FRACTURE → COLLAPSE → TERMINAL. Driven by NLI thresholds. Discrete and deterministic.

07

Minimax Algorithm

The Immortal Game (1851). Position evaluation per move. SOFT improves. AGGRESSIVE damages. At eval ≤ −7.5 — CHECKMATE.
