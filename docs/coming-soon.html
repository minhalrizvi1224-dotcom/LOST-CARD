What is LOST CARD?
LOST CARD is a daily relationship tool built for real life — not therapy, not advice columns, not vague motivation.

It is a computational simulation of how trust decays in conversation. You play as yourself. The other person responds based on real psychological data. The system tracks your Devotion, Excitement, and Presence — three cards that hold a relationship together — and shows you, in real time, which ones you are losing and why.

You can use it to understand:
  • Why a conversation with your mother always ends the same way
  • Why your best friend feels distant even when nothing happened
  • Why saying the right words still feels like the wrong choice
  • What your communication pattern actually looks like in data

This is not a game you win. It is a mirror you look into — and walk away from knowing yourself a little better.
A note from the developer
I am S. M. Minhal Abbas Rizvi — a student of Computer Science (BSSE) at MAJU, and I have been studying Cognitive Psychology independently for 8.5 years.

LOST CARD began as a theory — a way of explaining why relationships fail not because of hatred, but because of small, repeated miscommunications that compound silently until one day, there is nothing left to say.

That theory became "The Bet of Belief" — a book I am writing, due to be completed in 2028. LOST CARD is its first living proof: the theory, translated into 7 data structures from computer science, running as a simulation you can interact with.

I built this because I believe most people are not bad at relationships. They are simply unaware of the patterns they are repeating. LOST CARD makes those patterns visible.
