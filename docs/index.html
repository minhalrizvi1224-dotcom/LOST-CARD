A Computational Model of Relational Belief Decay

What Is LOST CARD? (Start Here)

Imagine you are playing a card game - but the game is your relationship with someone you care about. You have three cards in your hand:

💜
DEVOTION
How much you love and invest in this person
💙
EXCITEMENT
Your energy, curiosity, and joy in the relationship
💚
PRESENCE
How attentive and available you are for them

Every time you speak - every single thing you say or don't say - you are playing a card. Some words strengthen the relationship. Some words damage it. And some cards, once lost, do not come back.

LOST CARD is a computer program that simulates this. You make choices - be kind (SOFT), be harsh (AGGRESSIVE), or go silent (SILENT) - and the program tracks exactly what happens inside both the relationship and the brain. It uses seven computer science algorithms to do this, all running at the same time.

The goal: make it through all 23 moves without losing any card. If you can - that is called SALVATION.

What LOST CARD Is NOT

The most common misunderstanding - and why it matters.

✗ It is NOT a chatbot.

ChatGPT, Claude, and other AI systems generate text. LOST CARD runs a simulation engine. The AI component (Hair Band) is approximately 10% of the system - used only to generate dialogue in Custom Mode. The other 90% is deterministic mathematics that no language model can replicate.

Ask any AI chatbot: "What is my cortisol stack depth right now? How short is my relational exit path? When will my EXCITEMENT card drop?" It cannot answer - because it has no engine underneath. LOST CARD can, because it is running one.

✗ WHAT AI CHATBOTS DO
  • Generate text based on patterns
  • Answer questions from training data
  • Have no persistent state between messages
  • Cannot apply deterministic rules to track state
  • Cannot run data structures simultaneously
✓ WHAT LOST CARD DOES
  • Runs 7 DSA structures simultaneously per move
  • Applies hard psychology rules - not probability
  • Tracks state across every move with precision
  • Drops cards based on deterministic code conditions
  • Evaluates chess position with minimax every turn

The key difference: When you make 2 consecutive AGGRESSIVE moves in LOST CARD, the EXCITEMENT card drops. Not because AI decided it. Not because a language model predicted it. Because an if-statement in the code enforces it - based on Gottman's research on conflict escalation. That is what makes this a data structures project, not a chatbot.

LOST CARD vs ChatGPT - The Real Difference

The most common objection. Here is the precise answer.

OBJECTION 01 - "ChatGPT gives better answers"

Wrong comparison. LOST CARD does not answer questions - it runs a mathematical simulation. Ask ChatGPT: "What is my cortisol stack depth? How short is my relational exit path? Will my EXCITEMENT card drop on the next AGGRESSIVE move?" It has no idea - because it has no engine. LOST CARD has one.

OBJECTION 02 - "It's just another chatbot"

The AI (Hair Band) is ~10% of the project. The other 90% is pure deterministic code: Dijkstra's algorithm recalculates your relational exit path every single move. A LIFO Stack tracks cortisol with a hard cap of 7. A Minimax engine plays The Immortal Game (1851) simultaneously. A Finite State Machine transitions your relationship between HARMONY → FRACTURE → COLLAPSE → TERMINAL. No chatbot does any of this. Calling LOST CARD a chatbot because it uses an AI component is like calling a game engine "just a chatbot" because it renders text.

OBJECTION 03 - "The AI can be manipulated"

The AI component is locked and sandboxed. It plays a role - a specific person in a specific relationship type. The simulation engine itself - the card logic, the NLI formula, the cortisol stack, the chess evaluation - runs in pure JavaScript. It cannot be prompted, jailbroken, or manipulated. The rules are in code, not in a language model.

The fundamental difference: ChatGPT generates the most probable next word. LOST CARD enforces hard rules derived from neuroscience and Gottman's research - rules that cannot be overridden by prompt, mood, or context. When your EXCITEMENT card drops, it drops. The code decided. Not the AI.

The Bet of Belief - The Theory Behind It All

"Every word we speak in a relationship is a card we play. We play without knowing we are in a game. The moment we make a wrong move - the card leaves your hand. Suddenly. Not gradually. Gone."

— S. M. Minhal Abbas Rizvi

Most people think relationships break down slowly - that you can feel it coming. But that is not how it actually works. The moment a heart decides something is over, it decides it completely. There is no partial decision. There is no warning. The card drops.

The Bet of Belief is a framework built on one insight: trust is not a feeling, it is a computational state. It builds, decays, and collapses according to rules. Those rules can be discovered. Those rules can be modelled. And once modelled - they can be understood.

LOST CARD is the computational form of that theory. It takes the invisible mechanics of human connection and makes them visible, measurable, and traceable - move by move.

How Does It Work? - A Simple Walkthrough

1
You Choose a Relationship Type
Best Friend, Partner, Family, Colleague, Rival, Ex - or use the scripted story of Umm-e-Laila and Hani (Default Mode). Each relationship type has different trust levels, stress thresholds, and emotional stakes built in.
2
AI Generates a Real Conversation
In Custom Mode, you enter the real names of two people and describe the situation. The AI simulates their actual dialogue - what they would say, how they would say it - based on the relationship type and your inputs. This is not a generic script. It is personalised.
3
You Pick a Move Every Turn
Three choices appear. You do not know which is SOFT, AGGRESSIVE, or SILENT - you only see the conversation options. Just like in real life, you choose without a label.
4
The Simulation Updates in Real Time
Every choice triggers all seven data structures simultaneously. Trust shifts. NLI rises or falls. Cards are at risk. A chess engine evaluates your position. The relationship either stabilises or moves toward collapse.
5
The Session Ends - You Get the Full Report
At the end, you receive a deep psychological and technical analysis - every card's status, every DSA structure's final state, the chess position reached, what caused each card to drop (if any dropped), and what would have saved the relationship.

Three Types of Moves - Every Word Classified

Every response in a conversation falls into one of three categories. LOST CARD detects which one you chose and calculates the neurological and relational consequences.

SOFT
Apology · Warmth · Acknowledgment · Vulnerability · Repair
PFC ↓ Cortisol ↓ Dopamine ↑
Kind words that reduce stress on both sides. The relationship heals - slowly.
AGGRESSIVE
Defensiveness · Dismissal · Blame · Escalation · Contempt
PFC ↑↑ Cortisol ↑↑ Dopamine ↓
Harsh words that spike stress and damage trust fast. Two in a row - the EXCITEMENT card is at risk.
SILENT
Withdrawal · Avoidance · Non-response · Minimal reply
PFC ↑ Cortisol → Mirror ↓↓
Saying nothing is still a move. Three silent moves - the PRESENCE card is gone. Silence is not neutral.

The Brain Formula - NLI (Neurological Load Index)

Every move updates a number called the NLI. It measures how much stress the brain is under at any moment. Think of it as a brain overload meter:

NLI = (PFC × 0.4) + (Cortisol × 0.4) + (1 − Dopamine) × 0.2
PFC Load (40%)
Prefrontal Cortex - rational decision-making. 0.0 = clear mind. 1.0 = overwhelmed.
Cortisol (40%)
Stress hormone. Builds with conflict. Drains the ability to repair. 0.0 = calm. 1.0 = flooded.
Dopamine (20%)
Reward and motivation. High = engaged. Low = losing reason to try. Inverted in the formula.
HARMONY
NLI < 0.30
Both people are calm. Repair works.
FRACTURE
0.30 – 0.70
Stress is building. Harder to fix things.
COLLAPSE
NLI ≥ 0.70
Brain is overwhelmed. Cards are dropping.
OVERRIDE
NLI ≥ 0.85
Amygdala takes over. Reason is gone.

7 Data Structures - Each One a Piece of the Mind

This is not a gimmick. Each structure was chosen because it genuinely maps to a real psychological mechanism. All seven run simultaneously - every single move updates all of them at once.

01
Weighted DAG + Dijkstra
Hippocampal Memory Network
22 nodes = 22 relational moments. Think of each node as a memory. Dijkstra's algorithm finds the shortest path to the Exit node - the path to disconnection. AGGRESSIVE moves erode the edges. The shorter this path gets, the closer the relationship is to ending. Complexity: O((V+E) log V)
02
LIFO Stack
Cortisol Accumulation
Unresolved conflicts stack up - literally. Maximum depth 7 (the nervous system's conflict capacity). SOFT moves pop the stack - but only if NLI is below 0.50. If you are too stressed, you cannot process the apology. Overflow at depth 7 = EXCITEMENT card lost. Complexity: O(1)
03
Min-Heap Priority Queue
PFC Choice Corruption
Your brain ranks choices rationally under normal conditions. But when NLI exceeds 0.40, the prefrontal cortex degrades. At NLI ≥ 0.85, the amygdala takes over - and the AGGRESSIVE option is automatically surfaced first in the queue. Not chosen. Surfaced. That is the corruption. Complexity: O(log n)
04
Singly Linked List
Default Mode Network / Longing
Lost relational memories are stored as orphaned pointers in a linked list. The memory wrapper is freed - but the moment* data itself is intentionally NOT freed. This is not a bug. It is the architecture of longing. A memory with no valid address, still occupying space in the mind. Complexity: O(1) insert
05
Hash Map
Sovereign Key / Identity
Some truths in a relationship are locked behind identity. Two protected memory segments exist - each behind a hash key. Only the person who holds the key can access them. O(1) average lookup. Some things in a relationship are only reachable through knowing who you are to the other person.
06
Finite State Machine
Relationship Phase Transitions
Relationships do not deteriorate gradually. They shift between states: HARMONY → FRACTURE → COLLAPSE → TERMINAL. The NLI value determines which state you are in. A state machine models this perfectly because these transitions are discrete - not gradual. You cross a threshold, and the phase changes. Complexity: O(1)
07
Minimax Algorithm
Consequence Anticipation
The simulation plays The Immortal Game (Anderssen, 1851) - a real chess match known for brilliant sacrifice and ultimate loss. SOFT moves improve position. AGGRESSIVE moves damage it severely. SILENT moves create passive disadvantage. If the evaluation drops to −5.0: CHECKMATE - relationship over. Complexity: O(b^d) at depth 2

When Do Cards Drop?

Cards drop when specific thresholds are crossed. These are not random - they are based on real psychological research into when people emotionally disengage:

💜 DEVOTION
→ AGGRESSIVE move while NLI is low (habitual calm-state aggression - the most damaging kind)
→ Trust drops below 0.55 while Dopamine is still above 0.70 (the blindspot: you feel okay while eroding them)
💙 EXCITEMENT
→ Stack depth reaches 4+ and another AGGRESSIVE move is made
→ Two consecutive AGGRESSIVE moves (back-to-back attack without repair)
💚 PRESENCE
→ Three or more SILENT moves total across the session
→ Three consecutive moves with NLI above 0.75 (sustained brain-flood - you are no longer present)

Two Simulation Modes

🃏 Default Mode
A scripted 23-move narrative featuring two characters: Umm-e-Laila and Hani. The dialogue is fixed and authored. You choose how to respond at each move. All 7 DSA structures are fully active. Best for understanding the system before going deeper.
✨ Custom Mode (AI-Powered)
You provide real names, genders, a scenario, and the relationship type. The AI generates a personalised conversation - what these two people would actually say. You can simulate conversations with friends, partners, family, colleagues, childhood friends, rivals, even an ex. The AI plays the other person authentically. This is the core feature of LOST CARD.
